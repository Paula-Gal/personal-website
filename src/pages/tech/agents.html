AI Agents

What are AI Agents and why do they matter?

What is an AI Agent

AI agents are autonomous software systems designed to perform tasks, make decisions, and respond to information without constant human input. Instead of following a fixed script, they can observe their environment, reason about what they see, and take actions that move them toward a goal.

How they work

Most AI agents operate through a simple cycle:

  • Perceive — gather information: text, data, sensor inputs, or API results
  • Decide — using LLMs or reasoning models, evaluate goals and choose the next action
  • Act — send messages, execute code, update data, or search the web
  • Adapt — some agents learn from outcomes, improving over time

This loop makes them far more flexible than traditional rule-based software.

Where they're used

  • Customer support — responding to questions automatically
  • Workflow automation — running tasks, triggering tools, managing operations
  • Research assistance — finding information, summarizing sources, tracking updates
  • Personal productivity — scheduling, organizing data, writing, planning
  • Software development — coding, debugging, testing, and deploying autonomously

Why they matter

  • Save significant time by automating repetitive digital work
  • Increase efficiency for teams and individuals
  • Operate continuously, even when humans are offline
  • Enable new capabilities — autonomous workflows, self-running systems
